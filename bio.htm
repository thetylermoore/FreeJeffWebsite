"Free Jeff? Yeah, I remember those guys...

Once upon a time (mid 2000), two high school students named David Parris and Jeff Chandler, disillusioned with their high school’s current music scene, sat at their desks in US History. In their boredom they forged a radical idea, an idea for a new tomorrow. Only one word came to mind: “SKA.” Now, at the time, both of our heroes were involved in other high school bands: the entity known as BOVINE and THE SASQUATCH, both of which had not accomplished more than a few garage practices. It was from these ashes that the proverbial Free Jeff phoenix was formed.

With Jeff’s early departure in June of 2001, the band was faced with large horn-related problems. But the band has been able to trudge onward and upward. The current lineup features the talents of David Parris on lead vocals and guitar, Tyler Moore on lead guitar and backing vocals, Doug Carter lends his melaphone, vocal and keyboard skills to the project, Kevin Ferguson slapping out the funky drum beats like its 1972, Matt Schultz toots his trumpet and presents his unique interpretive dance of the 80’s, and last…and least, Alex Wirth, the funkiest soul since Bootsy Collins on bass.

The band has spent over a year developing a large local following by playing numerous professional gigs, including birthdays, talent shows, bat mitzvahs, etc. The band has only progressed from there, with the release of the band’s first album, the JALF EP. According to David Parris, “It was crazy. We had marching band girls from all over the country asking for Doug Carter’s number.”

The band maintains their insanely energetic live performances with a rigorous practice schedule and has developed a live show and sound like no other band in the ska music scene.

And what of the name itself? There must be a long story with that one. It must have originated from somewhere. What does it mean? Be it animal, vegetable or symbol? Or it some sort of cry from the working class, to those tired of Bruce Springsteen attempting to represent the plight of blue-collar America? Could it be just another way to mock the shallow feeling of teenage rebellion, commonly bought and sold on MTV? The answer my friend is waiting in the wind. The answer my friend is…

The band is very grateful for the support of its loving and loyal fanbase. Free Jeff continues to have high hopes for their prolonged rocking. “I forsee us rocking until we finally keel over and die, like Mick Jagger.”
